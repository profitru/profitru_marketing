Warehouse Management Software for Amazon, Flipkart & Meesho Sellers in India

Selling on Amazon, Flipkart, other marketplaces (including Meesho), or your own store only works when the warehouse matches what those channels think you have in stock. That is the same fragmentation Profitru addresses on the homepage under the problem narrative—spreadsheets, mismatched stock, and claims that never meet finance. This note ties those ops to the margin story in our multi-channel profit guide.

What is warehouse management in eCommerce?

Warehouse management is inventory, catalog uploads, dispatch, returns, QC, and claim tracking in one loop. It only pays off when those events roll up to SKU-level truth—the same “one command centre” idea behind how Profitru works and what you get on the main site.

1. Inventory management

Overselling and silent stockouts usually mean the warehouse and the channel are on different timelines. Aligning counts is step one before you trust any margin model; see why one margin definition across channels matters once the same SKU sells in more than one place.

  • Track stock across SKUs and variants
  • Manage bundles (BOM)
  • Real-time inventory updates
  • Avoid overselling

2. Bulk product upload via CSV

Standard CSV workflows reduce copy-paste between portals. Profitru is built around bulk data in—no marketplace API required—so catalog and operations can stay in sync with how you already export from Amazon, Flipkart, or Meesho.

  • Single upload pattern for multiple marketplaces
  • Less manual rework between teams
  • Faster catalog management

3. Dispatch management

Dispatch is where customer promise meets floor reality. Cleaner handoffs back to inventory reduce “ghost” units that break profit models on Flipkart or FBA-style flows.

  • Picking & packing workflows
  • Shipment tracking
  • Order status visibility

4. Return management & QC

Returns destroy margin when they are not tied to QC and restock rules. For D2C-heavy brands, COD, RTO, and margin is the companion read; for marketplaces, pair operational returns data with the fee story in our Amazon profit guide.

  • Track all returns
  • Run QC checks
  • Identify resellable vs damaged items
  • Update inventory automatically

5. SPF & claim tracking

Claims only recover cash when they are linked to the same units and timelines as operations. For Amazon India specifically, settlements rarely tell the full SKU story—see what your payout row still hides—then keep claim status next to returns in one system.

  • Monitor claim status
  • Link claims with returns
  • Improve revenue recovery

How this maps to Profitru

Profitru is not a generic WMS label: it connects operational reality (inventory, returns, claims, uploads) to real profit per SKU, so finance and ops stop debating different spreadsheets.
